About Anthony Willis

I’m a Geography major concentrating in Urban Planning at SUNY New Paltz, and I run Cross Country for the Hawks. On the course, I think in splits: plan the first mile, surge in the middle, and finish strong. I approach cities the same way — build a good base, invest steadily, and make smart moves that improve communities over time.

My work explores how city layouts influence access to education, parks, transportation, and opportunity. I study redlining, the historical practice of denying mortgages and loans based on neighborhood demographics. Although these maps were drawn decades ago, their effects persist — influencing property values, pollution exposure, and even average life expectancy.

Urban planning matters because it determines who gets access — to housing, mobility, and environmental safety. My goal is to use mapping, design, and data to repair past inequities and promote more sustainable, equitable communities.

School: SUNY New Paltz
Major: Geography (Urban Planning)
Team: Cross Country
Interests: Transportation equity, climate adaptation, redlining research

Why this site? It visualizes how historical redlining still affects modern city planning — blending architecture, data, and design.
